03 · Before You Apply

Visa sponsorship does not always mean relocation support.

Many UK employers can sponsor Skilled Worker visas without covering a single relocation cost. Know the difference before you get your hopes up.

Relocation packages are more common for

  • Software Engineers
  • Data Analysts & Data Scientists
  • AI & Machine Learning Specialists
  • Cybersecurity Professionals
  • Cloud Engineers
  • Quantitative Finance Professionals
  • Engineering Roles
  • Senior Leadership Positions
  • Specialist Consulting Roles

Relocation support may include

  • Flight tickets
  • Temporary accommodation
  • Shipping allowance
  • Visa fee reimbursement
  • Immigration lawyer support
  • Settling-in allowance
Search job descriptions for: "relocation assistance available" · "relocation package" · "global mobility" · "international candidates welcome" · "Skilled Worker sponsorship available"

05 · What You Need to Earn (2026 Rules)

The number the whole application hinges on.

Official Home Office figures, correct as of mid-2026. Rules change often — always confirm current numbers at gov.uk/skilled-worker-visa before applying.

£41,700/yr

The minimum salary for a Skilled Worker visa is whichever is higher of £41,700 a year, or the specific "going rate" set for your occupation code.

A lower threshold of £33,400 can apply for new entrants — under 26, recent graduates, or those switching from a Student visa — and for roles on the Immigration Salary List.
Only your basic gross salary counts. Bonuses, commission, overtime, and pension contributions are not included, even if guaranteed in your contract.
06 · What It Costs You

Budget for the applicant's side of the bill.

Visa application fee — up to 3 yrs£819
Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS) — 3 yrs£3,105
Proof of funds required (unless employer certifies)£1,270
Estimated minimum total£5,194

Lower fees (£628–£1,235) apply if the role is on the Immigration Salary List. The IHS is charged per year of visa length, paid upfront in one lump sum. Your employer can certify your maintenance funds instead of you proving your own £1,270.

07 · How Sponsorship Works, Step By Step

Five steps from job offer to decision.

1

Job offer

You receive an offer from a company holding a UK Sponsor Licence.

2

Certificate of Sponsorship (CoS)

Your employer issues a CoS — an electronic record confirming your role, salary, and occupation code.

3

Visa application

You apply online within 3 months of receiving your CoS, and pay the application fee plus the IHS.

4

Documents & biometrics

Submit supporting documents and attend a biometric appointment, or use the ID Check app.

5

Decision

Processing usually takes around 3 weeks outside the UK, or 8 weeks for in-country applications.

09 · Red Flags — Spotting Fake Sponsorship Offers

Genuine sponsors never ask you to pay for this.

✗

Any request for payment to "secure" a CoS or visa

Genuine sponsors never ask candidates to pay for this.

✗

Job offers with no real interview

Or interviews conducted only by chat with no video call.

✗

Pressure to pay an agent or "processing fee"

Before you've even signed a contract.

✗

Employer not on the official register of licensed sponsors

Always check before accepting.

✗

Salary or role details that change after you accept

Especially if they drop below visa thresholds.

Always verify a company on the official register of licensed sponsors before paying for anything or sharing personal documents.
